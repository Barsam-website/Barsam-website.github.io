About the Book

This book tells the true story of a mother; a story of love, loss, and the courage that caregiving demands every single day.

When Negin and her family welcome their rainbow baby, Barsam, they enter a world of hospital corridors, sleepless nights, and countless sacrifices. Amidst a rare genetic diagnosis, repeated ICU admissions, and difficult choices, they gradually discover a deeper strength‚Äîone that emerges from kindness, empathy, and a sense of purpose.

This book is both a love letter to a child and a companion for those walking a similar path. Written with honesty and clarity, it shares the story, offers practical ideas for daily living, and provides concise, actionable guidance for friends and communities who want to help.

Most importantly, it shows how hope can be reborn after profound grief. This is not merely a story of suffering, but a testament to love's endurance.

About the Author

I am Negin Salimi, born in Tehran to a Kurdish family, and for many years I have stretched my roots between two lands: Iran and the Netherlands.

I have been living in the Netherlands since 2009 and today I am an Assistant Professor at Wageningen University; a place where research, inspiration, and life interweave.

In 2013, I married my husband, Jafar Rezaei, in a union where love and thought grew side by side.

I am the mother of four children: Pedram, Behrad, Roza, and Barsam. Two are with me, and two are in my sky.

For me, motherhood is not a simple word; it is a journey through love, loss, and hope.

Each experience has remade me, into a woman who has found, from the depths of pain, a new light for giving meaning to life.
